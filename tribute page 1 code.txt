      <h2>Introduction</h2>
      <p>Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She was born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, Pakistan.</p>

      <h2>Early Life and Education</h2>
      <p>Malala was born into a Sunni Muslim family in Pakistan's Swat Valley. Her father, Ziauddin Yousafzai, is a poet, and ran a chain of schools in the region. Malala attended one of his schools along with her two younger brothers.</p>

      <p>Malala became an advocate for girls' education when she was 11 years old. She began writing a blog for the BBC under a pseudonym, detailing her life under Taliban rule and the importance of education for girls.</p>

      <h2>Activism and Attack</h2>
      <p>In 2012, Malala was shot in the head by a Taliban gunman while riding a bus to school. She was airlifted to a hospital in Birmingham, England, where she underwent surgery and received treatment for her injuries.</p>
      <img src="C:\Users\Dell\Desktop\web development oasis\malala yus.jpg" alt="Malala Yousafzai" width="800" height="533">
